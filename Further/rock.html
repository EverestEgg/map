This is probably the central feature of Newport Lakes. The rock bridge is fun for all ages, racing and jumping on the rocks is a fun past time. But it is also a great spot to sit and watch the waterbirds and the baby fish, though most of the fish are invasive species like Carp. The invasive species are one of the bigger issues with current conservation effort, including the fish, there are also foxâ€™s and cats that will kill wildlife.

While looking pristine and natural, these lakes are the remains of quarry pits that remain carefully managed. Sitting at about 3m deep the lake has a clay liner at the bottom to prevent water loss and a bore at the bottom managed by the council ensures the lake remains at a consistent level. Though none of this is particularly evident from just looking at the lake.
