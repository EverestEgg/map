Field Recording

The friends of Newport lakes are the local volunteer conservation group dedicate to the park. Established in 1997. They organise monthly working bees in the park to help current conservation efforts and improve the park. They provide advice to the council about their managements and future for the Lakes. And monitor the water quality, bird sightings and the general health of the park.

As the managements and conservation of the area is not complete, and maintaining the park requires constant work. Many friends of groups exist around the world, providing valuable small scale community lead conservation, within the local area there are Jawbone Reserve, Kororoit Creek, Skeleton Creek all have similar friends of groups.
